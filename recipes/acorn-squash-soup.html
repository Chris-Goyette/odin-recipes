Acorn Squash Soup

Sweet, nutty acorn squash and fresh, creamy Shamrock Farms Heavy Whipping Cream come together to create this warm and inviting soup, perfect for a crisp fall evening. For some crunchy contrast, we added some homemade sourdough croutons as garnish.

Ingredients

  • SOURDOUGH CROUTONS
    • 3 tbsp. unsalted butter, melted
    • 1 tsp. sweet paprika
    • Pinch of Kosher salt
    • 4 c. cubed sourdough bread (¾-inch cubes)
  • ACORN SQUASH SOUP
    • 3 acorn squashes (1 1/4 pounds each), halved and seeded
    • Pinch of Kosher salt
    • Freshly ground black pepper
    • 4 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil, divided
    • 1 medium yellow onion, finely chopped
    • 3 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 tsp. fresh thyme leaves, plus more for serving
    • 4 c. low-sodium chicken stock
    • 1/2 c. Shamrock Farms Heavy Whipping Cream, divided
    • 1 tsp. Worcestershire sauce
    • 1/8 tsp. ground nutmeg
    • Large pinch cayenne pepper
    • 1/3 c. grated Parmesan

Steps

  1. Make the croutons: Preheat oven to 325˚F. In a large bowl, combine butter, paprika, and a large pinch of salt. Add bread cubes and toss to coat. Sprinkle with cheese and toss again. Spread in an even layer on a baking tray. Bake, tossing a few times, until golden and crisp, 15 to 20 minutes. Let cool completely.
  2. Make the soup: Increase oven temperature to 425˚F. Drizzle cut sides of squash with 2 tablespoons oil, season with salt and pepper, and arrange cut-side down on a baking sheet. Bake until fork-tender, 25 to 30 minutes. Flip cut-side up and let cool slightly. Scoop roasted squash out of skins, discarding outer skin. Reduce oven temperature to 325˚F.
  3. In a large pot, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons oil over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, and thyme and cook, stirring occasionally, until tender, about 5 minutes. Pour in the broth and add the scooped squash, Worcestershire, nutmeg, and cayenne. Increase heat to high and simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Turn off the heat. Using an immersion blender, blend soup until creamy. Return heat to medium-high and add the Parmesan and ¼ cup Shamrock Farms Heavy Whipping Cream. Cook, stirring, until soup is smooth and hot, about 2 minutes more.
  5. Ladle soup into bowls, drizzle with remaining heavy cream, and top with croutons and more thyme leaves.
